About Me

I am an Assistant Professor of Economics at the Strome College of Business, Old Dominion University. My work focuses on public economics, urban economics, and health economics, with a particular emphasis on homelessness, housing programs, and the effectiveness of safety net policies such as Medicaid and the Earned Income Tax Credit.

My research integrates administrative and geospatial data to answer policy-relevant questions and provide evidence for policymakers working to address societal challenges related to housing, health, and inequality.

Research Interests

  • Urban Economics
  • Public Economics
  • Health Economics
  • Housing Policy
  • Homelessness & Safety Net Programs

Publications & Working Papers

Selected works include:

  • Housing, Homelessness, and Urban Policy Analysis
  • Effects of Medicaid and Peer Support Programs on Health Outcomes
  • Impacts of the Earned Income Tax Credit on Household Spending
  • Property Value Effects of Homeless Shelters (King County, WA)

Teaching

I teach Urban Economics (ECON 445W/545W), Macroeconomics Principles, and Managerial Economics. I incorporate real-world content, spatial data, and modern empirical methods to help students apply economic concepts to policy issues.
